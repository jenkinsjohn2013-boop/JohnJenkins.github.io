Robotics · Controls · Haptics · Senior Capstone
Teleoperation of a UR3 with a Haptic Feedback Device

Designed and developed a delta-geometry parallel robot as a 6-DoF input device to teleoperate a Universal Robots UR3 collaborative arm with real-time haptic force feedback. The delta base uses capstan drives with brushless DC motors to provide 3-DoF translational force feedback with minimal backlash; a spherical wrist captures 3-DoF rotational input. I was primarily responsible for the controls system and kinematics software: implementing forward kinematics for the delta input device using Teensy 4.1 encoder readings over USB, solving inverse kinematics for the UR3 arm, constructing the full end-effector transformation matrix, and streaming six joint angles to the UR3 at 125 Hz via RTDE over ethernet. Haptic force boundaries are rendered by applying torque commands through Voltage Torque Control on the capstan drives.

Python Forward / Inverse Kinematics RTDE (Real-Time Data Exchange) Teensy 4.1 Voltage Torque Control SolidWorks Brushless DC Motors Capstan Drives
